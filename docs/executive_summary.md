# Ice Cream Sales Analysis — Executive Summary

## The Business Challenge

An ice cream business sought to understand what drives sales to optimize inventory, staffing, and marketing decisions.

---

## Four Questions Investigated

1. What is the most popular flavor?
2. How does temperature affect sales?
3. How do weekends and holidays affect sales?
4. How does profitability differ for new vs returning customers?

---

## Key Findings at a Glance

| Finding | Business Impact |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Lemon is #1** (713 units, 34% share) | Prioritize lemon inventory to avoid stockouts |
| **Temperature drives sales** (R² = 0.98) | Use weather forecasts for inventory/staffing planning |
| **Hot days = +148% sales** | Stock 2.5x more when temps exceed 80°F |
| **Weekends don't boost sales** | No extra staffing/inventory needed |
| **Holidays slightly lower (-5%)** | Investigate cause; don't overstock |
| **Customer data unavailable** | Implement tracking for future analysis |

---

## The Bottom Line

**Temperature is the #1 lever.** A simple practice of checking the 5-day forecast and adjusting inventory/staffing accordingly would have the highest impact on operations and revenue.

---

## Immediate Action Items

1. ✅ Integrate weather forecasting into weekly inventory planning
2. ✅ Ensure lemon flavor is always fully stocked
3. ✅ Remove assumptions about weekend/holiday demand spikes
4. 🔄 Begin collecting customer data for future segmentation analysis

---

## Data & Methods

- **Dataset:** 2019 daily sales, temperatures, and weekly flavor sales
- **Tools:** Microsoft Excel (SUMIFS, AVERAGEIFS, WEEKDAY, scatter plots, trendlines)
- **Analysis:** Correlation analysis, group comparisons, derived column creation
